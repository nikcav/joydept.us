About Joy Dept.

Joy Dept. believes in inspiring joyful human interactions. When we think of joy, we tend to think of wonderful people and the meaningful experiences we’ve shared with those people. The stuff of life.

Joy Dept. is working to create more of this “stuff.” More curiosity. More exploration, delight, meaningful experiences...more joy!

But joy isn’t always so easy to come by. It’s often a do-it-yourself project. Joy requires an open mind, inspiration, stimulation, safety, and comfort. And if you’re not feeling inspired, or worse yet, if you’re uncomfortable, you’re not very likely to be glowing with joy. That's where we come in!

Campsite Installation


Picture this: It’s a cool summer evening, nestled in the Colorado wilderness. You hear gentle pops and crackles coming from the fire...the familiar muffled laughter of your closest friends...crickets welcome the night. Someone cracks open a can of beer. You smell sweet, damp grass, the earthy pine logs in the fire, and toasted marshmallow smooshed into dark chocolate and graham crackers. The moonlight illuminates colorful tents, and a light breeze carries the fire’s warmth over your body. You can’t help but to smile.

We want to recreate this familiar experience for more people. We’re merging a public art installation with an interactive experience that will bring people together, first by curiosity, then with delight, and then, through connection.

This one-of-a-kind interactive installation, themed like a campsite, will build and scale as more people participate. As more people join in, nature’s sounds will amplify, the fire’s flames will grow bigger and brighter, and other thematic stimuli will surprise and delight.

Meet the Team

We're a group of grad students trying to create a little more joy in the world!

Technology Lead & Web Design

Nikki Cavalier is Joy Dept.’s resident artist and creative technologist. In addition to her aesthetic skillset, she possesses hidden talents that include binge-watching and sleeping for long intervals. She’s happiest curled up in warm, sunny spots.

What does Nikki miss most about elementary school? Coloring! Also, being able to be instant best friends with someone just because you both like the same color or cartoon character. Nikki’s goal in life is to keep designing cool shit that makes people smile and fills them with curiosity.

If he could incorporate one aspect of elementary school into his adult life, it would be naptime for sure, though recess and playing with friends comes in at a close second. In Cameron’s opinion, being able to watch someone enjoying something he created is the best reward.
